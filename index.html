Assignment 1: Video Editing

Assignment 1: Video Editing

The first assignment required students to create a completely new storyline with only two video resources: one for the visual aspect, and the other for the voiceover. Despite these limitations, I had wanted to create what could be described as a combination of "Home Alone" and the "Mandela Catalogue".

The original video "Alone at Home" espouses safety at home, being an instructional video for children left alone in their house. I wanted to create the opposite: that no matter how safe one perceives their home to be, there is still a threat. Ans so I created a story about a town haunted by something, and a little boy who was taken away by 'something' because they were left all alone in their house.

The already existing footage from the video resource I had chosen was very in-line with the story I was going to present. As the original video was about 14 minutes long, there was an ample number of scenes I could select to use as part of my assignment. I had first selected the scene of what I would like to have as the climax, which would be the most important scene for my project, before moving on to select the remaining scenes to build around it. This allowed me to create a narrative relatively quickly, as all I had to do was then create an introduction leading up to the climax, and an aftermath.

I wished to create a cohesive storyline, and so both the audio tracks and video edits used could not be too 'separate'. For example, there cannot be any comical sound effects when there shouldn't be any. The entire piece should be very plot driven, a straight linear path from scene A to scene B. Both sound and voiceover were used to set the emotions of each scene. Sound can be very influential in the emotional state of the person watching your story. For example, in the beginning scene of my project, I had a girl stand up from a table and walk over to a door. If it was just the original audio of the scene or complete silence, nothing would seem wrong with the situation; a girl is just walking over to a door because she heard something. But because I chose to have a segment cheerful music playing in the background - one that quickly fades as there's knocking heard - it creates a suspenseful mood to the scene. The audience can now sense that there's something wrong, that whatever's on the other side of the door is 'bad'.

At first, the edits make the pacing slow. I had kept the scenes long to slowly build the tension. However, as the video enters the climax of the storm scene, there is faster edits, there are many cuts in a few seconds, to make the scenes seem more frantic. After the climax, the edits slow down again and the tension dies back down.

I wanted to create a juxtaposition between the climax and the other scenes. The storm scene is dark and suspenseful. It is the climax of the entire project and needs to stand out against every other scene. And so, it is the scene with the most amount of layering in the audio too. Everything during this scene is maximised in order to best convey that the storm scene is the important one; that here is where things don't look so good for the boy. Contrastingly, the scenes before and after the storm scene is very minimalistic. Very little audio is used, and what is used is always quick to die down. the scenes are also light, appearing to be daytime as opposed to the storm scene's night, to further enhance the contrast between the two. It is very much a horror-within-a-sleepy-little-town vibe, in which most characters except a select one (or few) are unaware of the horrors within their town.

Assignment 2: Icon Design

Assignment 2: Icon Design

The second assignment required students to create a set of 9 different icons and their corresponding audio upon interaction. Several different concepts were given, and we were to choose one of them to base the icon designs after.

I originally wanted to choose the concept of 'dream'. However, I had quickly realised that 'dream' would be too complicated, and anything I drew would be very detail-heavy, so I had instead chosen the concept of 'growth', since no one was apparently doing that one.

I wanted to portray three different themes all linked to the concept of growth. With growth, one would most often think of the natural world, so I wanted to portray things that grow in the sky, on the ground, and in the water.

Many different iterations were created to portray the concept of 'growth'. Ultimately, I did land on growth in the natural world, since it is the theme that can most easily be conveyed the concept between the icons.

In order to create my icons, I simply sketched out everything in Adobe Photoshop first, before transferring them to Adobe Illustrator and tracing over everything with the line and curvature tools and adding colour.

As we need to have a set of 9 icons in total, I decided to divide them into rows of three representing growth in 'Earth', 'Water' and 'Sky'. For all three rows, I chose to have the background square shaped. Analogous colours (blue, teal/turquoise, green) were used in order to appear more unified and a common theme of lines running across each background was added. For the 'Earth' themed icons, they were to have rigid brown lines, for 'Water' themed they were to have wavy blue lines and for 'Sky' themed they were to have swirly white lines. I also tried to keep the art-style as similar as possible, so one icon doesn't look too simplistic or complicated next to the rest.

To further enhance the concept of growth, I had made it so that people can visually see the growth. For each Icon, once clicked, a change can be seen, such as a lotus flower growing in height, or a butterfly coming out of its chrysalis.

For the sound portion of this assignment, seeing as I had my icons transition from one appearance to the other, I made sure each sound clip I had chosen for the audio design matched with the sound normally heard in the context of each icon scene. For the plant I used the sounds of leaves rustling, for the bird with a nest I used the sounds of an egg cracking and birds chirping. Ultimately, I wanted the audio to as closely match with the scenes depicted in the icons as much as possible.

Assignment 3: 3D Environment Design

Assignment 3: 3D Environment Design

The third assignment required students to create a 3D environment using the game engine Unity. We had to have a 'parti' to form and influence our environment - the two must be connected.

This project was so, so much harder compared to the previous two, especially since I unfortunately suffer from cybersickness, so that makes it all the more difficult. With 2D media, All I had to focus on is how something appears front on. However, with the 3D environment, I had to figure out how everything looked from all sides and directions.

Having a working prototype throughout this assignment was vital to the completion of my 3D environment. It helped me get the general idea of what my project was going to turn out like visually and as an interactive space. It also helped me understand which assets need to be changed or moved so that I (and those who play the game) are not clipped out of the world and into the void.

One compulsory aspect of this assignment was the virtual camera. The virtual camera was to act as the point-of-view of a person in a gallery. It was to portray the POV of an outsider looking into the life of someone else. It was similar in function to the role of the camera in Assignment 1. However, whereas the camera in the first assignment is completely removed from the situation occurring in the video, the camera used in Assignment 3 is still within the 'area'. It is interactive - although you are still an outsider looking into another person's life, you have the choice to see what you look at.

Sound is another crucial part of this assignment, and its implementation is vastly different from the previous assignments. Sound in Assignment 1 is loud. It is a constant thing with only very brief periods of silence. It is easy to implement, as all one must do is to take pre-recorded clips of audio and add them to video edit. Sound in Assignment 2 is quieter and is only used to enhance the concept behind the icons. Compared to Assignment 1, it was slightly harder to implement, as one must include it into the coding of a website, as well as link it to the icons to ensure that the audio is played when triggered the correct way. For Assignment 3, however, sound needs to be three dimensional as well. You need to be able to hear the same sound from multiple directions and not just one, and sound in the 3D environment had to operate in a similar fashion to sound in the real world.

For my presentation method, I had chosen to display the 3D environment as an art gallery. Every scene that depicts my 'parti' is seen first behind either a painting frame or a glass case. For the frames that you can enter, the scenes behind them are all fairly cartoon like and scaled not so realistic to enforce the idea of these scenes being a person's memory, and like most memories, distorted and coloured by the owner's own perception of events.
